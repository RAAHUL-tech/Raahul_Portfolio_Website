DATON (Detection And Tracking of Number Plates)

November 2023 – Jan 2024 | Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, Torchvision, YOLO


This project is built using Python, OpenCV, and TensorFlow for real-time detection and tracking of vehicle number plates. Developed as part of the AI Club at Puducherry Technological University, the primary objective is to create a deep learning model capable of recognizing and tracking the number plates of vehicles entering and leaving the university premises. Information such as the entry time and license number is stored in a CSV file.
The prototype operates by processing a video input, which in a real-world scenario would be provided by a CCTV camera installed at the campus entrance. The YOLO V8 nano model is employed to detect cars, trucks, bikes, and other vehicles in the video frames. Each vehicle is then assigned a unique ID using the open-source SORT library, implemented in the sort folder. Once IDs are assigned, the next step involves detecting the vehicle's license plate using a pre-trained model called WPOD-NET, which is capable of recognizing number plates from eight different countries, including India. After detecting and cropping the license plate, the text is extracted using Python's EasyOCR library, along with a confidence score. Finally, the extracted results are stored in a CSV file.

Neural Style Transfer Using Transformers

Jan 2024 – May 2024 | Python, PyTorch, OpenCV, Torchvision


Neural Style Transfer (NST) has significantly evolved since its introduction with the pioneering work by Gatys et al. in 2015. This method uses Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to blend the content from one image with the style of another, revolutionizing artistic image creation and impacting various domains such as media, fashion, and design.Existing systems predominantly employ CNNs and have started incorporating transformer architectures for style transfer, which facilitate better handling of long-range dependencies critical for capturing detailed artistic styles. However, these systems, including those using the Content-Aware Positional Encoding (CAPE), face challenges with computational efficiency, particularly when processing complex styles or high-resolution images. To address the limitations of CAPE and enhance system scalability and efficiency, the proposed model integrates Pyramidal Positional Encoding (PPE) with a transformer-based framework. PPE improves upon CAPE by incorporating a hierarchical, pyramid-like structure that captures spatial information at multiple scales. This method simplifies the encoding process, reduces computational demands, and enhances the model's ability to generalize across different artistic styles and larger images, making it more adaptable and faster. The proposed system also leverages reinforcement learning (RL) to optimize the stylization process dynamically. By using RL against the traditional loss function, the system can adjust its parameters in real-time based on the immediate feedback received from the output's quality assessment. This method significantly accelerates the learning process, enabling quicker convergence and allowing the system to produce higher quality stylized images with reduced computational resources. After 4000 epochs, content fidelity improved significantly to 2.0685, reflecting a reduction of content loss by 62.6% from initial values. Similarly, the system reduced global effects, or style loss, by 57.4% to 0.8578. The inference time is impressively fast at just 1.39 seconds. Moreover, the integration of the Reinforcement Learning (RL) algorithm further enhanced the system, reducing content fidelity to 2.0308 and global effects to 0.7473, with a minimal impact on inference time, which increased slightly to 1.40 seconds.
